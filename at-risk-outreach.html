About The Program

Over the past 7 years The Station has partnered with several inner-city/at risk youth organizations. We have hosted Harbor House (inner city Oakland), Outdoor Outreach (inner city San Diego), HerShe Foster Girls, and more. And we are continuously looking for more organizations to partner with to create an incredible camp experience for underprivileged youth.


At The Station, we have a HUGE heart to reach out to these kids. There are many at-risk and underprivileged youth who never had or may not have the opportunity to participate in a really fun camp experience let alone partake in some of the best adventures of their lives. Our passion is to see these individuals experience life in ways they never have before that will ultimately change them forever!


If you want more information on how you can help, click here. If your organization would like to partner with us in hosting a summer or winter camp for these youth, let us know!

Scholarships


It's such a bummer when kids can't experience camp simply because they don't have money. Their passion and sense for adventure is there, as well as a desire to escape from their daily situations but often times they can't because of the financial costs.


We are committed to making sure kids from inner-city organizations have the amazing opportunity to come to camp. We don't see them as undeserved youth because we believe they are more than deserving of such an opportunity and know that an experience at The Station will be forever life-changing. That is why we have set up a scholarship fund to help alleviate the costs for these kids to come to camp.


Think about when you were growing up and all the cool things you didn't get to do: go to a ball game, buy some candy, have your very own snowboard or skis. Now think about the millions of kids who feel this way on a daily basis. Pretty heavy, huh? It doesn't have to end there! YOU have the ability to make a difference!


If you or your business would like to make a tax-deductible donation to the scholarship fund, please let us know or make a donation. Every little bit helps and will make it that much more possible to send a kid to camp!

Food & Lodging

what to expect


There is nothing worse than rock climbing or hiking after a bad night’s sleep, on a poor diet or both. We do our best to make sure campers are fully rested and fed so they can go all out the next day. Our comfortable cabins come furnished with plush mattresses, forced air heating and full bathrooms. As well, our camp chef has been heralded by many past campers as their favorite staff member. Campers will feel like they are eating home-cooked meals even if they are hours away from mom and dad. Nothing is better after a day outdoors than amazing comfort food!﻿
